Facts about Autism Spectrum Disorder

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

is a lifelong neurological and developmental disability that affects how people communicate, interact, learn, and behave. Symptoms usually appear in early childhood and present uniquely in each individual, earning it the name "spectrum".

Key Facts & Statistics:

Prevalence:

ASD affects about 1% of the global population, with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) noting that it is identified in roughly 1 in 36 children in the U.S.

Gender Disparity:

Boys are nearly four times more likely to be diagnosed with ASD than girls, partly because symptoms often present more subtly in females.

No Cure:

There is currently no medical cure or blood test for ASD, but early behavioral, speech, and occupational therapies can significantly improve a person's daily life skills.

Scientific Consensus:

Extensive research has definitively proven that vaccines do not cause ASD, and it is not a result of bad parenting. The condition is complex and primarily driven by a combination of genetic and early brain development factors.
